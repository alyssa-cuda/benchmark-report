A Paycard Adoption Story
The shift from 100% instant pay in January to ~35–40% by H2 reflects paycard gaining traction as a preferred payment option following its launch — not a move toward slower payment rails. As more options became available, contractors chose paycard. This is a product expansion story, not a regression.

Section 03 — Worker Engagement
Worker Engagement

Most workers come back. The question is how many don't.

30-day return rates above 84% sound strong — until you see the one-and-done rate underneath. Between 17 and 28 percent of first-shift workers never work a second shift. That's a platform's biggest retention opportunity.

~88%
return within 30 days of their first shift

The 30-day return rate improved from 79–84% in Q1 to 91–95% by Q3/Q4 — a double-digit gain within the year. Workers who engaged early kept engaging.

The one-and-done rate — workers who complete a first shift and never return — ranges from 17 to 28% across mature cohorts. For a platform adding thousands of workers monthly, this is the conversion problem that compounds fastest.
